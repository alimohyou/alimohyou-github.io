Digitalization

The way businesses run and engage with their customers has been drastically changed by the digital economy, which has also brought about a number of positive effects like more convenience, efficiency, and creativity. But this move to a more digital economy has also meant increased security concerns, especially with regard to cyberthreats and weaknesses. I will analyse the security implications of the digital economy in this answer using the writings of Wei et al. (2019) and Spremic and Simunic (2018) as a basis, along with my personal observations and ideas.

An important cyber security issue that comes with becoming a completely digital organisation is the higher danger of cyberattacks. According to Spremic and Simunic (2018), organisations are now more susceptible to cyberthreats like ransomware attacks, malware, and hacking since business processes and data are becoming more digitalized. Furthermore, because digital systems are interconnected, a single breach may have far-reaching effects and can impact a huge number of stakeholders.

There exist supplementary cyber security obstacles for a physical small and medium-sized firm (SME) seeking to transition into a digital enterprise. These could include the requirement to purchase new hardware and software in order to support digital operations, as well as a lack of technical know-how and resources. These difficulties might be especially severe for smaller businesses since they do not have the same resources and finance as larger corporations, as noted by Spremic and Simunic (2018).

In a nutshell, the digital economy has significantly benefited businesses and created new opportunities, but it has also resulted in new security implications and concerns. The implementation of strong security measures across all organisational aspects is necessary for a completely digital firm, as it presents more cyber security concerns. Becoming a digital firm can be especially difficult for brick and mortar SMEs, and more resources and knowledge could be required to make sure that security concerns are adequately handled. Lastly, given the current energy crisis, it's critical to weigh the advantages of the digital transformation against the need to cut back on energy use and lessen environmental effect, as well as the sustainability of the digital economy.

Ref:
Spremic, M., & Simunic, D. (2018). Cybersecurity challenges in the digital economy. Interdisciplinary Description of Complex Systems: INDECS, 16(3-B), 432-442. Wei, X., Chen, X., Li, Y., & Zhang, J. (2019). Cybersecurity risks and challenges of a fully digital enterprise. Journal of Business Research, 98, 365-375.
